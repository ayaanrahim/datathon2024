2 Year Future Sales Forecast

Purpose and Functionality: We developed a machine learning model that aims to predict sales, total selling prices, and product quantities for the next two years. The model leverages historical sales data to provide insights into future trends, which may help the company with inventory management, marketing strategies, and overall sales planning. Model Description: Algorithm Used: The model utilizes a Random Forest Regressor, a robust ensemble learning method that combines multiple decision trees to enhance predictive accuracy. Features: The model includes lag features (past sales, selling prices, and quantities) and moving average features over different windows (3, 6, 12 months) to capture temporal trends and seasonality. Target Variables: The model predicts three main targets: Total Sales Total Selling Price Total Quantity Model Performance: Accuracy: The accuracy of the model is evaluated using the Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE), which measures the prediction accuracy as a percentage. MAPE Scores: Total Sales: 5.2% Total Selling Price: 4.8% Total Quantity: 5.0% These MAPE scores indicate the average percentage error between the predicted and actual values, showing the model's reliability in making accurate predictions. Predictions: The model provides detailed forecasts for the next two years, broken down into monthly intervals. Key predictions include: Total Company Predictions: Aggregated sales, selling prices, and quantities for the entire company. Product-Level Predictions: Detailed predictions for individual products, helping identify best-selling items and those with declining performance. Company-Level Predictions: Sales forecasts for different company divisions, aiding in strategic resource allocation. Country-Level Predictions: Sales forecasts for different shipping countries, supporting targeted international marketing strategies. Key Insights from Predictions: Seasonal Trends: Sales are predicted to peak during the spring months (April and May), aligning with historical trends. Product Performance: Certain products (e.g., Prod_1009) continue to show high sales, while others may need reassessment. Geographical Variations: High-performing regions like Georgia and California are expected to maintain their sales volumes, necessitating focused inventory and marketing efforts.
